The Mechanics of Photocatalysis

The intervention grid utilizes a heterogeneous photocatalytic process. When ultraviolet (UV) light illuminates the Titanium Dioxide (TiO₂) coating, it generates electron-hole pairs. These highly reactive species interact with water vapor and oxygen in the air to produce hydroxyl radicals (•OH) and superoxide anions (O₂⁻).

These powerful oxidants react with Nitrogen Dioxide (NO₂) molecules, converting them into non-toxic nitrates (NO₃⁻) which are adsorbed onto the surface of the grid, effectively removing the pollutant from the air stream.

The STM32 microcontroller acts as the nervous system of the grid. By continuously monitoring environmental parameters, it dynamically adjusts the UV intensity to maintain optimal reaction rates while minimizing energy consumption. This closed-loop control ensures that the grid remains effective across varying urban conditions, from high-traffic peak hours to stagnant evening air.
